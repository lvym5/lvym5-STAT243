Univariate Nonbinary Categorical Tests

Univariate nonbinary categorical tests: Known as Chi-Squared tests

Chi squared tests test for whether values are equally likely (or fit, according to their predicted percentages)

Hypotheses:

  • H0: pa = some proportion, pb = some proportion. etc
  • Ha ≠ some proportion (or at least one of the proportions is off)

We can use a Chi-squared test statistic with formula:

$$\chi^2 = \sum \frac{{(O - E)^2}}{E}$$

Given our test statistic, we can use technology or R to find the p-value

  • Pchisq(test stat, df)
  • Degrees of freedom is (number of rows - 1) * (number of columns - 1)
