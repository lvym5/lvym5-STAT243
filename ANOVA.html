ANOVA

ANOVA: One way ANOVA
ANOVA uses
One way ANOVA is a method that allows us to compare multiple means based on one variable. It is used to determine whether two samples' means are significantly different or not.

Examples:

  • Comparing mean weight loss of different groups using different workout methods
  • Comparing mean sales from different types of promotions
  • Comparing mean stress levels after conducting varying therapeutic methods on different groups

    • Why is ANOVA helpful?
    ANOVA is used when we have a single variable (also called a factor), but we want to investigate different variations and measure the differences on the dependent variable.

    Calculations

    Variables:

  • Let k = number of groups
  • Let n = sample size
  • df1 = k-1
  • df2 = n-k
  • F-stat = MSG/MSE

    • Grand mean x̄: ∑xi/n, where x̄ is the mean of all observations.

    Parameters:

  • H0: μ1 = μ2 = ... = μn
  • Ha: At least one of the means is different

    • SSE: Sum of Squared Errors (sum of observations - observation's group mean)2
    SSG: Sum of Squares Group (group means - grand mean)2*ni
    MSG: Mean squared variation due to groups (SSG/df1)
    MSE: Mean squared error (SSE/df2)

    Form of the ANOVA Table:

    Source SS DF MS F
    Group SSG df1 MSG MSG/MSE
    Error SSE df2 MSE
    Total SSG + SSE Total df SSG+SSE/total df
