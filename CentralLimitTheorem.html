The Central Limit Theorem

Central Limit Theorem: The fundamental theorem of statistics
What is the Central Limit Theorem?

The Central Limit Theorem tells us that our random independent variables, sampling distribution, and sample mean leans towards the standard normal distribution (bell shaped curve), even if the original variables are not distributed normally.

Mathematical Rules of the Central Limit Theorem

If Y = aX -> μy = aμx, and variance is a2σx2

If Y = x+b, then μy = μx + b and variance is σ2y = σ2x

  • An iid sample is an independent and identical distribution (to simulate this, use resample() in R)

  • An srs is a simple random sample, equally likely, but no replacement (use sample() in R)

    • E(aX + bY) = aE(x) + bE(y)

    Var(X+Y) = Var(x) + Var(y) if x and y are independent
